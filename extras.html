Here's some other fun stuff I've done.

Groovin' The Moo X Collarts 2019.

Planned and executed video interviews with a variety of popular artists at Groovin' The Moo Bendigo 2019 around my chosen topic of gender diversity within the music industry. Edited the footage into a range of different 'Take 5' segments for use by both Collarts and Groovin' The Moo for marketing purposes in future.

jem hollway · Walk With Us: Australian Women Talk Getting Home

"Walk With Us: Australian Women Talk Getting Home"

This audio piece is something I made in response to the Eurydice Dixon murder in 2018. Featuring the voices of over 10 female presenting people, this piece aims to outline the fear faced by women as they walk home at night. This piece was featured in the Monthly Hr. Podcast by The Monthly Magazine.

Maggie Beth Launch Campaign

This video is one of four that I had the pleasure of creating for Naarm based creative Maggie Beth for the launch of her new earring line. The videos are short, snappy and designed to tell a story whilst still being easily shareable via social media.

A Hitch To The Sticks

Over the course of three years (2018-2020) I have been working seasonally for A Hitch To The Sticks festival to help capture footage for promotional videos like this one. I work both behind the camera as well as in front of it, and lend my skills in both the planning and editing process.

Hitch TV.

Hitch TV. is a segment of the videos we make for A Hitch To The Sticks. In these, I work to direct the set-up of the shots, write the questions and execute the live interviews in high pressure environments. Artists I have interviewed include the likes of Ella Hooper (Killing Heidi), Tim Rogers, Tex Perkins, Stonefield and more.

Sass The Patriarchy

I worked over Sass The Patriarchy 2019 to produce a range of videos both in the lead up and post festival. These included interviews with artists (Thando, Simona Castricum), capturing live footage of the event, and posting to social media.

Backyard Festivals: The Future of the Garage Band

I produced this little video journalism piece to outline the importance of local music in local spaces. This was produced and edited by me in 2018 as part of my Entertainment Journalism diploma.

HUNNY

I was lucky enough to work alongside the team at HUNNY (A night to celebrate gender diversity in music) in capturing some footage from the night. This is one of the little lo-fi clips I made in the follow up of the event.

Syn 1700

I spent a season working both producing and conducting live interviews with local artists on Channel 31 as part of the crew at SYN 1700. These were aired live on Monday nights.
